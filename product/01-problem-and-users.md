# Problem & Users

## User segments

### Segment A: Consumers (self-serve event hosts)

- Jobs-to-be-done:
  - Quickly build and book an event experience (DJ/Karaoke/Photo Booth) for a specific event date/time
  - Understand estimated pricing and what’s included before committing
  - Reduce vendor back-and-forth and uncertainty
- Context:
  - Planning a one-off event; time-constrained; high emotional stakes (weddings, milestones)
- Constraints:
  - Limited planning experience
  - Budget uncertainty; needs transparent estimates
  - Needs confidence/trust
- Existing tools:
  - Google/Instagram/Yelp/The Knot/etc., spreadsheets/notes, email/SMS/DMs

### Segment B: Event planners (professional or semi-pro) (later)

- Jobs-to-be-done:
  - Coordinate vendors across multiple events
  - Track quotes, availability, and confirmations at scale
- Context:
  - Multi-stakeholder coordination; deadlines; reputation risk
- Constraints:
  - Needs speed + reliability + documentation

## Current workflow (as-is)

1. Search online directories/social and ask for recommendations.
2. Send many inquiries (often unstructured; repeating details).
3. Wait for replies, then follow up repeatedly.
4. Collect quotes in a messy mix of email/SMS; compare manually.
5. Confirm availability; handle deposits/contracts separately.

## Pain points

- Discovery is fragmented; quality signals are inconsistent.
- Inquiry is high-effort (repeating details, inconsistent responses).
- Quotes are hard to compare (different formats, hidden fees, unclear availability).
- Booking trust is low (fear of mismatch/no-shows).

## Risks if we get it wrong

- Users won’t trust pricing transparency or what’s included.
- Vendors won’t adopt if leads are low-quality or workflow is burdensome.
- Too much friction in booking kills conversion.
